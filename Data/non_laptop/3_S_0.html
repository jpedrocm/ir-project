Notepads

Notepads are essential for every home, school, law firm and office. Whether it's a design with standard lined paper or a specialized style with unique features, the right option makes work easier and more efficient. Shop the variety available at Staples to find the one with the exact features needed for any task.

Benefits of Notepads
There are numerous options available, including multiple sizes, colors and types of paper. Choose from blank sheets, ruled paper, graph paper and message pads. Some styles are designed to work as journals to keep notes securely in one place. Others have perforations and carbon copies so notes can be easily exchanged with other parties. There are styles designed to fit into a three-ring binder and others with spiral binding. Some have an adhesive binding and perforations that allow pages to tear off easily. Most designs have white or yellow paper, though you can also find options with brightly-colored pages and decorative headers. Small novelty notepads work well as party favors or branded trade show items. Styles with a magnetic backing stick onto the fridge, so they're ideal for keeping a grocery list or missed call messages.

Choose the Right Notepad for the Task
The point of a notepad is to increase organization, so it's essential to choose the style and size that will work best for each particular task. Legal pads are 8.5 inches x 11.75 inches and are extremely common in law firms and other offices. The lines make it easy to keep neat and organized notes, and the design of the pad keeps everything together while also allowing individual pages to tear out cleanly. Steno notebooks with a coil-lock binding along the top and Gregg-ruled paper are also popular. Many people prefer a slightly smaller ruled notepad, so there are many options measuring approximately 5 inches x 8 inches. There are also 3-inch x 5-inch pads that fit comfortably in a pocket or handbag.

What Notepads are Best for an Office?
Legal and Steno pads are both common in offices. They have clear, simple lines and work well for taking notes. Receptionists may also use a "missed call" pad with fields to clearly indicate who called and write down any messages. These pads often include carbon copies. There are also receipt pads with areas to write down services rendered, amount charged and payment given in duplicate or triplicate. These are extremely useful for contractors, repairmen and other technicians.

Are There Special Notepads for Students?
Students may find legal pads useful along with three-hole-punch writing pads. Engineering or graph paper is another popular style, especially for students taking math or science classes. It's usually quad-ruled with evenly-spaced vertical and horizontal lines. Some styles are double sided, with bright ruling on one side and faint lines on the other.

Are There Notepads Designed for Outdoor Use?
Tactical pads are designed for outdoor use and have water-resistant pages in between durable, waterproof, polyethylene covers. Most styles have a spiral binding and are 3 inches x 5 inches for easy storage in a backpack or pocket. These pads are extremely useful for surveyors, mechanics, military personnel and hunters.
